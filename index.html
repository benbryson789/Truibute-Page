Grace Hopper

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was an American computer scientist and United States Navy rear admiral.
Perhaps her best-known contribution to computing was the invention of the compiler, the intermediate program that translates English language instructions into the language of the target computer.

    Here's a time line of Grace Hopper:

  • 1906 - Born in New York City, New York
  • 1928 - Graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Vassar with a degree in mathematics and physics
  • 1930 - Master's Degree at Yale
  • 1930 - Married New York University Professor Vincent Foster Hopper from 1930-1945
  • 1934 - Phd in mathematics from Yale
  • 1938 -
  • 1943 - Enslisted in the United States Navy Reserves.
  • 1947 - She served on the Mark I computer programming staff headed by Howard H. Aiken.
  • 1949 - Hopper became an employee of the Eckert-Machly Computer Corporation as a senior mathematician and joined the team developing the UNIVAC I
  • 1952 - Grace Hopper and her team created the first compiler
  • 1959 - Helped develop COBOL -- one of the first high-level programming languages
  • 1985 - Appointed Read Admiral in the United States Navy
  • 1991 - First female recipient of the medal of technology
